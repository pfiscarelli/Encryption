Code Wheel Instructions
In cryptography, a Caesar cipher is one of the simplest and
most widely known encryption techniques.

It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter in plaintext
is replaced by a letter some fixed number of positions down the alphabet.

For example, with a left shift of 3, D would be replaced by A, 
E would become B, and so on.
